**The Enemy Within**

The invisible enemy bares its fangs against us,
It is within all of us, eating away at our insides,
well hidden but always close by.
It chips away at our souls and erodes our meaning and existence,
slowly but surely, and at different rates for each and everyone of us, pushing us closer to our ideological deaths,
at every waking moment and even in our sleep.

Some people, with their mediocre aspirations,
for their whole life,
never get to notice it's existence while it's at it's work;
for the machinations of the servant of entropy are potent but well-hidden from undiscerning eyes.
For those victims: no matter how mediocre their lived-lives, no matter how ordinary their aspirations,
it was an extraordinary achievement to be blessed in terms of fortune;
these people were lucky enough to die peacefully as they slept, never actively experiencing, the harrowing process of them being slowly eaten away - before being devoured completely;
they died peacefully in their sleep as their dreams wept.

More than it enjoys feeding,
it enjoys a process of hide and seek; a process that is reserved for a different breed of prey.
The ones that dared to dream,
but were unfaithful.
They took a wrong turn while trying to take a shortcut,
and that's how they lost their way.
Now every turn they take is a wrong turn:
It's these ones whose insecurities taste the most delicious and their final desperation - moments before they break down - make the whole chase worthwhile and meaningful.

It's ironic,
that how the one that destroys meanings, is trying to justify it's existence,
and trying to find its own meaning in proving to it's victims that "it was wrong to dream, do you see it now?".

Toying with it's prey as it tries to escape,
it pollutes it's mind to always look for an easy way out, while it predicts it's every move as it tries to escape it's fate.

To make the hunt more entertaining, it allows its prey to narrowly escape simple traps, each one an imperfect creation, but nonetheless more elaborate and burdensome than the last, all the while luring it closer towards its perfected creation: the final trap, where this magnificent beast of chase will finally reveal it's presence to devour it's victim, a dish prepared meticulously by this master chef, following a recipe of disaster, with it's ingredient that has now been cooked to perfection.

**The Slow Descent into the Abyss**

Trying to escape your destiny,
you sealed shut your fate.
Trapped yourself in a room while running around in circles,
going around everywhere, but also going nowhere.
You tried to fool yourself, but you fooled nobody;
a clown, of your own making,
gaining nothing and losing everything.

It's that damned room where the predator and the prey finally meet.

You noticed it's existence even before it revealed itself.

You knew it all along,
that something was wrong.

There was this lingering feeling in your heart, the gut feeling that became stronger every time you kept failing in your pursuits,
that someone kept messing up your plans in the background; your plans, no matter how meticulous and well crafted, always failed to materialize......almost as if something sinister was cooking up trouble. After failing many times over and over, you don't even see the point of trying anymore. What good would a half-hearted, unmotivated attempt gonna do, when all those prior attempts ended up in a failure.

The dreams that have long lost their light,
can illuminate your path no longer,
as you keep spiralling into deeper darkness.
Surely you must have lost your way,
as in trying to achieve your dreams you have lost something crucial - yourself.

**The Awakening**

The trap is too perfect to allow the prey to escape, yet you try anyways: same old trio of triggers, habits and familiar feelings - you choose the easy route again: run - run from everything that feels difficult, uncomfortable, dangerous, that makes you feel anxious and just hurts you more and more and pushes you towards this swamp of endless sadness continuously.
It immediately relieves the anxiety, and strengthens your resolve to escape - if you can just run away from this hell, maybe next time you may find a situation that is easier than this, maybe one day you may actually be able to pull through your weight...maybe....just maybe.

But...something feels off about today...
this damn trap is just too perfect -
there is no safe corner, there is no weaker, easily destroyed section,
just unbreakable, endless walls that enclose you from all directions, stretching without limits, yet also seem to be somehow closing in on you, continuously reducing the zone that allows free movement and increasing your anxiety.
Then suddenly the realization hits....you are gonna be crushed by the walls as the trap tightens.
It makes you think...was it all a deliberate plan, an elaborate scheme by someone to lure you here?
The earlier imperfect traps incrementally pushed you towards this exact final destination - the perfect trap.
All the conditioning the past traps did on you - that you always choose to escape first thing as soon as things get difficult - kept fueling your escapist tendency and slowly chipped away your resolve to push through things - to actually pick yourselves up and fight.
Think..think...
So if the enemy wants me to run, what if I choose to not run....what happens then?
Can not running be the true solution to this?

**The Reclaiming of the Light**

The damned room was not a trap forged by the enemy.
It was the place where the enemy finally ran out of shadows to hide in.

You stood there, chest heaving, the walls of your own making closing in. The predator circled, fangs bared, whispering the same lie it had fed you for years:
*"You have sealed your fate. Dreams were never meant for you."*

But this time you did not run.

At first, you only stood.

Your legs shook. Your mind screamed the old familiar song — *just one more shortcut, one more delay, one more easy way out*. The voice was so familiar it felt like home. You had listened to it a thousand times before. It had always been kinder than this moment.

*"Why fight?"* the voice whispered inside your head. *"You've already lost so many times. Look at you — cornered, exhausted, broken. Just let it end. It will be quieter that way."*

You almost took a step back. Almost.

But there was nowhere left to step. The room had no door anymore. The walls were your own accumulated failures, your shortcuts, your abandoned dreams. You had built your own cage so perfectly that even the predator seemed surprised you had finally stopped running.

A cornered mouse, you thought bitterly, has no choice but to bare its teeth.

The thought was small. Tiny. Barely a spark. But it was the first one in years that did not come from the enemy.

You looked at the beast again. For the first time, you did not look away.

*"I'm scared,"* the voice inside you admitted — honest, trembling, but still yours. *"I've always been scared. But I'm tired of running from something I can't even see clearly."*

The enemy lunged.

You did not flinch.

Its claws met something it had never encountered in you before — not strength, not courage yet, but the simple refusal to look away. The first thread of illusion tore. A mask slipped. You saw, for a fraction of a second, that the colossal juggernaut with a thousand heads and limbs was not solid. It was all smoke and fog, composed of memory fragments composed of every moment in time you had chosen the easy path and called it fate.

The enemy recoiled, surprised.

You felt the burn in your chest again — not the old familiar dread, but something quieter. A small, stubborn ember that had refused to die no matter how many times you had tried to smother it with excuses.

*"Maybe I don't have to be fearless,"* the voice inside you continued, softer now, almost wondering. *"Maybe I just have to be willing to be afraid and still stand here."*

You took one step forward.

The enemy hissed and grew larger, wrapping itself in new shadows, new failures, new proofs that you were unworthy. But each time it struck, you noticed something strange — the blows landed, yet they no longer landed as deeply. Because you were no longer running. You were looking.

And in looking, you began to see.

The thousand heads were echoes of your own doubts.
The limbs were the shortcuts you had taken and the dreams you had abandoned.
The fangs were the lies you had told yourself so you could stay small and safe.

The enemy was powerful only when you fled from it.
When you faced it, it was only an illusion wearing a borrowed crown.

You took another step.

*"I'm still scared,"* the voice inside admitted, honest as ever. *"But I'm more terrified of betraying my dreams forever than I am of facing this monstrosity."*

The ember in your chest flared brighter. Not a bonfire yet — just a steady glow. Enough to illuminate the next step. Enough to see the next thread of illusion.

The enemy roared, but its voice cracked. For the first time, it sounded… uncertain.

You kept walking forward.

One slow, trembling step at a time.

Each step unraveled another layer of the mask. Each step proved that the predator had never been undefeatable — it had only been unexamined.

The room that had felt like the end of everything began to feel like the beginning of something else.

The invisible enemy bared its fangs one final time, but the fire in your heart — small, stubborn, and newly tended — burned too steadily. The fog lifted completely.

With a shriek that sounded strangely like relief, the entropy agent fled — not destroyed by force, but **unmasked and rendered powerless**. It could no longer hide in the corners of your mind. It could no longer swap your puzzle pieces, because you now saw the full picture and refused to let illusion rearrange it.

You stood in the center of the room.

The walls were still there, but they no longer felt like a cage.

You looked at the ember in your chest — the last remaining dream that had refused to die — and for the first time in years, you did not turn away from it.

You cupped your hands around it.

You began to nurture it.

Not with grand promises or desperate vows.
Just with the quiet, daily choice to stop running and start tending.

The invisible enemy had not been defeated by a hero.
It had been defeated by a man who finally stopped believing its lies and chose to face it anyway.

And in that choice, the long night began to end.

The dreams that had lost their lustre slowly started to glow again — not because the darkness had vanished, but because you had finally learned how to tend the light even while the darkness watched.

You were no longer the one who ran.
You were the one who stayed, and tended, and chose — afraid, but no longer fleeing.

And that was enough.

The story was no longer the enemy's.
It was yours.

And it had only just begun.
